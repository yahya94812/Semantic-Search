# Reading Notes — Climate Science & Policy

**Source:** IPCC Sixth Assessment Report (AR6), 2021–2023

## Section 1: Physical Science Basis

### Current Warming

Global surface temperature has increased by approximately **1.1°C** above pre-industrial
levels (1850–1900 baseline) as of 2011–2020. This warming is unequivocally caused by
human activities, primarily fossil fuel combustion, deforestation, and agriculture.

### Key Indicators of Change

- Arctic sea ice has declined at a rate of **13% per decade** since 1979.
- Global mean sea level rose by **0.20m** between 1901 and 2018.
- Ocean heat content has increased in all ocean basins.
- Permafrost thaw is accelerating, releasing stored methane (a potent greenhouse gas).
- Extreme weather events (heatwaves, heavy rainfall, droughts) have intensified.

### Emission Scenarios (SSPs)

| Scenario  | Description                  | Warming by 2100 |
|-----------|------------------------------|-----------------|
| SSP1-1.9  | Very low emissions           | ~1.5°C          |
| SSP2-4.5  | Intermediate (current trend) | ~2.7°C          |
| SSP5-8.5  | High emissions (BAU)         | up to 4.4°C     |

## Section 2: Mitigation Strategies

### Energy Transition

- Solar and wind costs have fallen **85%** and **55%** respectively since 2010.
- Electrification of transport and heating reduces fossil fuel dependency.
- Green hydrogen is a promising carrier for hard-to-abate sectors (steel, shipping).

### Nature-Based Solutions

- Reforestation and afforestation can sequester up to **3.6 GtCO2/year**.
- Peatland restoration prevents release of large stored carbon stocks.
- Mangroves and blue carbon ecosystems are highly efficient per hectare.

### Carbon Capture

- **DACCS** (Direct Air Carbon Capture and Storage): expensive (~$300–600/tonne today).
- **BECCS** (Bioenergy + CCS): land-use trade-offs with food production.
- Required in almost all 1.5°C pathways per IPCC models.

## Section 3: Policy Landscape

### Carbon Pricing

As of 2023, **73 carbon pricing instruments** are in place globally, covering ~23% of
global emissions. The EU ETS price hit **€100/tonne** in 2023.

### Just Transition

Decarbonisation must address equity — fossil fuel workers and vulnerable communities
need retraining programs, social safety nets, and economic development support.
